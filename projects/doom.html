Doom Meets Resident Evil

Solo Project • GZDoom / UDB • Released • 2025

Overview

After using UDB for months and making mods i decided to step up and make something bigger. I wanted to make something that had never been done before. Fuse the iconic survial horror inside the fast paced doom gameplay. The result is a mod thats unique and offers a gameplay to the iconic doom from 1998. The player must explore once again the police station, find his way, defeat enemies and unfold the mystery.

After publishing the mod, it attracted more than 250 players something that im extremely proud of and never thought possible.

Playtime: ~45–90 minutes • Difficulty: UV / Nightmare friendly • Focus: Fast-paced arenas, tight flow, hidden secrets and satisfying combat loops.

Workflow

  • I started by collecting references from the original Raccoon City Police Station to capture its authentic layout and atmosphere. Once I had a solid visual foundation, I moved to GZDoom and began blocking out the main areas of the map. After establishing the complete structure, I started placing gameplay elements specifically the keys and key items required for the player to make progress through the map. Next, I placed enemies in strategic positions to create challenging and varied combat encounters. Finally, after several playtesting sessions, I carefully adjusted and placed ammo, health packs, and other resources to balance the overall experience and pacing.

Features

Classic Doom action combined with survival horror from the resident evil

Added new twists and mystery to the original design for a new experience

Detailed and carefully crafted police station.

Personal Experience and Challenges

  • This project was an extremely valuable experience for me. I set a clear deadline of 8 weeks and committed to it, which forced me to plan carefully and stay highly motivated throughout the entire development process.
  • The strict timeline kept me constantly engaged and pushed me to improve the project every day. During development I was continuously researching new techniques, iterating on my designs, and learning advanced game design and level design practices along the way.
  • My biggest technical challenge was working with outdated software. To create a believable two-floor environment, I had to treat the second floor as a completely separate map and teleport the player between them — a creative workaround that taught me a lot about engine limitations and smart level architecture.
  • The greatest design challenge was maintaining consistent flow and pacing. I wanted to keep surprising the player with new elements, mechanics, and moments of tension throughout the entire experience. Playtesting and external feedback would have been extremely helpful, but since I was working solo, I had to rely entirely on self-experimentation and repeated self-playtesting to refine the balance and feel.
