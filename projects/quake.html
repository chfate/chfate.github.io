Quake Meets Silent Hill

Solo Project • TrenchBroom• Released • 2025

Overview

After making Doom Meets Resident Evil i wanted to challenge myself and do one big project in a different engine, now Trenchbroom. After making multiplayer maps inside the software and getting the hang of it i decided to make a single player mod this time set in the Silent Hill Universe.Trenchbroom offered more possibilities to get creative and i used this to my advantage to make the difficult task of not only implementing the Base version of midwitch highschool, but also the hellish version of it too, that made Silent Hill the hit that is today.I followed the same formula as my doom mod, explore the school,solve puzzles, find ways to progress and fight enemies.

Playtime: ~25-30 minutes • Development Time : 8 weeks

Workflow

  • Started by collecting references from original Silent Hill games (fog, rusty textures, distorted architecture)
  • Created blockouts in TrenchBroom for all maps, focusing on claustrophobia and disorientation
  • Placed key items, enemies and sound triggers to build tension and pacing
  • Iterated heavily through self-playtesting to adjust horror beats and flow
  • Balanced secrets, exploration rewards and combat encounters for replayability

Features

  • Psychological horror atmosphere with dense fog, ambient sounds and unsettling visuals
  • Exploration-heavy maps full of secrets, hidden notes and environmental storytelling
  • Survival mechanics blended with classic Doom combat (limited ammo, tension over action)
  • Multiple endings based on discovered secrets and player choices

Personal Experience and Challenges

  • This was my most ambitious solo horror project — setting strict deadlines helped me stay focused and motivated
  • Learned advanced techniques in atmosphere, sound layering and psychological pacing through constant iteration
  • Biggest technical challenge: engine limitations for fog and dynamic lighting — had to use creative workarounds
  • Main design challenge: building sustained tension without external feedback — relied on self-playtesting and repeated experiments
