The Forbidden Hike

A Firewatch-Inspired Exploration Level

School Project 3 Weeks Unity

Summary

Inspired by Firewatch, you are tasked with keeping the area safe from fires and any other dangers that might arise. What begins as an afternoon adventure soon takes a wrong turn‚Ä¶

The core design goal of this level was to shape a clear emotional progression through spatial design. The player begins in a position of safety and clarity, gradually transitioning into uncertainty, tension and isolation.

Rather than relying only on narrative exposition, I focused on how verticality, visibility, environmental contrast and pacing could influence the player‚Äôs emotional state throughout the experience.

Narrative Context

In pre-production I always start by asking questions to define the level characteristics.

References and Inspirations

  • My main inspiration came from my home country Greece and its vastly unexplored environments. I wanted to showcase real life locations to achieve realism.

Mechanics / Design Goals

Mechanics:

  • Jump
  • Crouch
  • Interact

Design Goals:

  • Emotional Pacing: Calm ‚Üí Curiosity ‚Üí Tension ‚Üí Isolation.
  • Narrative-Driven Guidance: The radio reinforces safety early and creates isolation when removed.
  • Spatial Contrast: Open vistas create comfort, narrow passages create vulnerability.

Level Walkthrough

Beat 1 (Start ‚Äì Safety & Clarity)

  • The player is informed through the radio to go look for a potential fire. (Objective/Goal Started)
  • He is in the watchtower and can see the whole playable area. (Vista)
  • Through the narrative guidance from the radio, he chooses the shortcut to reach his goal. (Player Guidance)
  • The voice foreshadows the jump, so the player is prepared to take it (Teach Mechanic/Tutorial).
  • After the jump he can't backtrack, meaning the player must push forward. (One-way valve)

Beat 2 (Middle ‚Äì Curiosity & Rising Tension)

  • The player now sees the objective visually (smoke) and progresses inside the Forest. (Tension rising)
  • He sees a locked gate and must find the key. (Player Engagement)
  • Visual indication draws him to investigate the building. (Frame the area)
  • He finds out that the building has been breached. (Unease)

Beat 3 (End ‚Äì Isolation & Unease)

  • The gate opens revealing scattered props. (Tension)
  • The radio stops responding. (Isolation)
  • Environmental storytelling escalates. (Emotional peak)

Final Thoughts

  • Play with verticality. The player starts elevated and gradually descends, reinforcing the emotional shift.
  • Environmental contrast and leading lines guide the player organically.

Overall, this project taught me the importance of iteration, real-world inspiration, and subtle environmental storytelling to enhance emotional impact.
