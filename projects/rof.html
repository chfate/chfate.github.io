Witcher III: Revenge Of Ofir

Team Project • 25 Members • RedKit • In Development • 2025–2026

Overview

Witcher III: Revenge of Ofir is an ambitious fan-made expansion that transports players to the mysterious eastern empire of Ofir. This complete overhaul delivers a brand-new experience, featuring an entirely new explorable region, original quests, new characters, expanded systems, and deeply integrated rich lore. The mod stands shoulder-to-shoulder with the official DLCs in scope, quality, and ambition, offering a worthy continuation of The Witcher 3’s world.

Status: In active development • Estimated full release: 2026

My Role & Contributions

This was a collaborative project with a team of 25 members. My role was strictly **Level Designer**. I focused exclusively on level design tasks and did not contribute to scripting, quest writing, dialogue, companions, combat mechanics, UI, asset creation, or other areas.

Level Design Tasks I Handled

  • Blockout and multiple iterations of key explorable zones in the Ofir region inside RedKit
  • Implementation of leading lines, landmarks, vistas and environmental storytelling techniques
  • Researching, finding references and implementing them inside the engine

All screenshots and videos on this page showcase **only the level design work I personally contributed**. All other elements (quests, dialogue, characters, combat, scripting, new systems) were handled by other team members.

Level Design Contributions

Within the project, I personally led the design and iteration of two major areas:

Abandoned Village POI

I was tasked with creating one of the many Points of Interest in the game: the Abandoned Village.

First, I asked questions to define the story of this place. I came up with the idea that this village was once a bustling trade hotspot, but a sudden attack by ghouls forced the inhabitants to flee. Now it stands empty and overrun by monsters.

My main goal was to make the abandoned feeling stand out, so I added dried vegetation (dead trees, grass, and bushes) to emphasize isolation. I also integrated half-buried houses in the sand to make the location feel more realistic and unique.

After implementing the level space, I added props to reinforce the fiction: half-buried wagons outside the village (breadcrumbing the player to approach), a half-buried well in the middle of the trade area, and decoration props to show the active trade that existed before the abandonment.

Final Result

Main Village – House Interiors

  • Designed and detailed interiors for multiple houses in the main village
  • Focused on functional and thematic interior layout (living spaces, storage, hidden compartments)
  • Integrated narrative elements through furniture, notes, and lighting to support quest hooks
  • Balanced scale, readability, and immersion to feel authentic to Ofir’s culture and architecture

Key Features

  • New explorable region: Ofir with unique deserts, bazaars and eastern architecture
  • 20+ hours of new quests with branching choices and multiple endings
  • Recruitable companions and new political factions
  • Expanded crafting, alchemy and combat systems
